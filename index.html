🎓 Help & Tutorial Guide

▶️
Take the Guided Tour
A 2-minute walkthrough that clicks through every section for you
→
Getting Started
📊Dashboard▾
Your home screen when you log in. Shows quick stats (total students, average performance, pending tasks) and charts giving you an at-a-glance view of the whole school. Click any nav item on the left to dig into details.
Main
👨‍🎓Students▾
The full student directory.
  1. Use the search box or grade filter to find a student quickly.
  2. Click a student's row to open their profile card (photo, contacts, results history).
  3. Use Add Student to create a new record, or the edit icon to update an existing one.
📋Results & Grades▾
Where subject marks are entered and reviewed per term.
  1. Pick the grade and term you want to work on.
  2. Enter or edit marks per subject for each student.
  3. Use Compare Terms (↔️ icon) to see how a class or student improved between terms.
📈Analytics▾
Visual charts comparing class averages, subject performance, and trends over terms — useful for spotting which subjects or grades need attention.
🚨At-Risk▾
Automatically lists students scoring below 41% so teachers can follow up quickly. The red badge on the nav item shows how many students currently need attention.
🔍Data Quality▾
Scans all records for problems — missing marks, scores that look like typos (e.g. way too high or too low) — so you can fix data before printing report cards.
👥Duplicates▾
Finds students who may have been entered twice — flags a record only when the UPI number, student name, and grade all match another record exactly.
🏅Grading System▾
A quick reference card explaining the CBC grading scale used across the portal — what score range maps to what grade/rating.
Management
📄Report Cards▾
Generate printable/downloadable report cards for a term.
  1. Choose the grade and term.
  2. Preview a single student's card, or generate for the whole class in bulk.
  3. Cards export as PDF and include each student's photo, subject marks and overall grade.
📥Import Data▾
Bulk-upload student records or results from a CSV/Excel file instead of typing them in one by one. The system previews the rows and flags any duplicates before saving. (Only visible to Advanced Admin accounts.)
📢Announcements▾
Post school-wide notices that all staff will see when they log in. Click New Announcement to write one.
💬Messages▾
Direct chat between you and other teachers/admins on the platform — real-time, like WhatsApp but inside the portal. Unread messages show as a badge on the nav item.
👤My Profile▾
Update your own display name/photo, and change your password here.
⚙️Settings▾
Admin-level configuration:
  • Firebase Configuration — connection details for the database (leave alone unless you know what you're doing).
  • Portal Settings — general options for the app.
  • Term Lock / Freeze — lock a past term so marks can no longer be edited, preventing accidental changes to finalized results.
  • Admin Account — manage who has admin access.
🗂️Audit Log▾
A running history of important actions taken in the system — who signed in, who edited a record, who imported data — useful for accountability.
Top Bar Shortcuts
🔍Smart Search▾
Press Ctrl+K or click 🔍 Search anytime to instantly jump to any student, result, or page — no need to navigate manually.
🌙Dark / Light Mode▾
Click the moon/sun icon in the top bar to switch between dark and light themes. Your choice is remembered next time you log in.
🔄Refresh▾
Data updates live in most cases, but if something looks out of date, click 🔄 Refresh to pull the latest from the database.

🏅 CBC Grading System

Scores are entered as raw marks out of 100 per subject. Each subject score is automatically converted into a Competency-Based Curriculum (CBC) Performance Level and a corresponding point value below. A learner's overall average is calculated from all subject scores, and the same scale is applied to determine the overall performance level shown on the report card and dashboard.

Score Range Level Points Meaning
How the overall grade is calculated:
1. Each subject's raw score (0–100) is matched against the table above to get that subject's level and points.
2. The learner's overall average = sum of all subject scores ÷ number of subjects.
3. The overall average is matched against the same table to determine the learner's overall CBC level (e.g. EE2, ME1) shown on dashboards, reports and rankings.
4. Class/grade-level analytics (EE/ME/AE/BE counts, at-risk lists, distribution charts) are built by tallying how many learners fall into each band above.
